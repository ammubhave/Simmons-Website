Housing Policy

Simmons Transfers

Between the fall and spring terms, the Housing Office will conduct a dormitory readjustment lottery for students transferring in and out of Simmons and communicate the results to the RACs. In addition, throughout the semester, the Housing Office may assign students to Simmons as vacancies arise. As students are assigned to Simmons, the RACs should assign them to rooms. These assignments are left to the discretion of the RACs, with the general guidelines that these assignments should be appropriate to the class year of the incoming student. Any student assigned to a room in this manner may not squat their room in the house-wide spring lottery.

Mini Lotteries

Each semester, after Registration Day, the RACs should conduct a Semester Readjustment Lottery. This lottery is designed to allocate any rooms which have opened due to withdrawals/transfers which have not been assigned to incoming students. If there are no such rooms, the RACs may elect not to hold the lottery. This lottery is open to any Simmons resident, though residents do not have to enter.

Students who elect to enter the lottery will have their selection order determined by a public, manual drawing. Residents may observe the lottery, though they do not have to be present. The priorities will be those of the house-wide Spring Lottery: Seniors, 9+ Term, Juniors, Sophomores, and Freshmen.

No sooner than the day following the selection order lottery, the RACs will hold the room selection process. When it is a student's turn, he may choose an open room (a single or unoccupied double) or he may remain in his room. If he chooses an open room, he is assigned to the chosen room and his old room is added to the list of open rooms (unless his old room is now a singly-occupied double). Once all the open rooms have been claimed, the lottery is closed.

Room Swaps

Students may swap rooms during a term. Everyone involved in the swap — including any roommates — must email the RACs <Address Obscured> to confirm that they approve of the swap. Once the RACs have approval from everyone, they will notify the entire house body by emailing the official housing mailing list <Address Obscured> to announce the proposed swap. Any resident then has twenty four hours to lodge any objections. These objections will be kept confidential, and if they are sufficient the swap will be refused. If there are no objections, the swap will go through and the students must move into their new rooms as soon as possible.

Special Circumstances

If students have conflicts or extenuating circumstances and need to move into a single or another room, the request must come from MIT Medical, MIT Counseling, or the Simmons House Team, comprised of the Simmons Housemasters and Associate Housemasters. No personal requests will be considered. Resolution of these issues is left to the house team and the RACs. Any student assigned to a room in this manner may not squat that room in the house-wide spring lottery.

House-Wide Spring Lottery: Selection Order Process

The house-wide spring lottery is designed to allocate rooms for the following academic year. It should occur as soon as possible following confirmation of the final housing lists from the Housing Office.

Two weeks before the room selection weekend, students may declare blocks. Any residents may block together, irrespective of gender or class year. The individual rankings of each resident in a block will be averaged together to give the ranking of the block. When the block's turn comes, each member of the block will pick their room sequentially, in the order of their original ranking. In the event of a tie in the rankings, preference goes to the individual over the block, and the smaller block over the larger. All blocks must be declared by midnight, the day before the selection order lottery, and are final once the selection order lottery has begun. No one may exit a block by squatting.

One week before the room selection weekend, the RACs should conduct the selection order lottery. This lottery must be public, though residents do not have to attend. Preferences in the lottery are:

  1. Resident Seniors
  2. Transferring Seniors
  3. Resident 9th Term
  4. Transferring 9th Term
  5. Resident Juniors
  6. Transferring Juniors
  7. Resident Sophomores
  8. Transferring Sophomores

The selection order lottery will establish the picking order within each of these broad groups. Once the lottery has been run, the RACs will publish the overall order.

The preferred method of conducting the selection order lottery is to have the names of all the students from a respective group be drawn from a hat. Once a slip is drawn, the pick number should be written on the slip in ink. The RACs then repeat this process for all 8 groups until all the names have been drawn. At the end of the lottery, the name slips must be preserved until the completion of the room selection process.

Room Squatting:

With a few exceptions, residents may squat their current rooms. If a resident declares a squat, they are removed from the lottery and assigned to their current room. Squats may be declared until the moment that another resident selects that room, though residents are asked to declare them as soon as possible. Declarations of squats are irrevocable. Students who were assigned to their rooms by the RACs may not squat their rooms. Additionally, the twenty-five smallest doubles are reserved for freshmen and may not be selected in the lottery or squatted by their current residents.

Additionally, anyone who has swapped rooms since the last April/May Simmons internal lottery process may not squat their room in the next lottery process. September/January internal processes are not to be considered swaps for the purposes of this rule; they are legitimate lottery-based rooming switches.

House-Wide Spring Lottery: Room Selection Process

On the room selection weekend, the RACs will proceed in order of the published rankings. Each student will be given one minute to select their room. If a student does not select a room in the period, or is not present at all, they will be dropped to the bottom of their group (i.e. Resident Seniors or Transferring Sophomores) and the RACs will move on to the next name on the list. At the end of each group, the RACs will announce the absent students. If the student again misses their turn, they will be dropped to the end of the lottery (i.e. after the transferring sophomores).

The list of residents will be broken into blocks of fifty students, with one hour allocated to each block of fifty. A block of fifty may start after the allocated hour, but will not begin before that time.

When a student's turn comes, he may pick any open room he wishes. If he selects a double, he may immediately designate a roommate. If he designates a roommate, the roommate is assigned to the room and removed from the lottery. If he does not choose a roommate, the slot remains open and will not be filled without the student's approval. If necessary, at the end of the lottery or during the Freshman Rooming Lottery, a student may be assigned to the open slot if no other openings are available.

If a resident is unable to attend the physical selection period, he may designate a proxy to choose instead. This proxy may be another student, who is given the power to choose a room for the resident, or the RACs. If the RACs are chosen to act as a proxy, the resident should submit a preference sheet, indicating the rooms/characteristics he would prefer. Anyone who declares a proxy must abide by the proxy's decision. Notification of intent to use a proxy must be emailed to the RACs <Address Obscured> no later than midnight the day before the room selection process.

The RACs should reserve rooms in the lottery for incoming CMI students as well as students returning from a leave of absence. These students should be considered transferring seniors and should be added to the end of the transferring seniors group. The RACs should select the largest rooms then available for these students, with a preference for scattering them throughout the building.

Fifty doubles, including the smallest twenty-five doubles in the building, should be reserved for incoming freshmen. The twenty-five smallest doubles may not be chosen in the spring lottery, and may not be squatted. The remaining twenty-five doubles reserved for freshmen shall be the last twenty-five not chosen in the spring lottery. Once all except twenty-five doubles (excluding the twenty-five smallest) have been chosen, no student may choose an empty double, and no student may squat one of the remaining doubles.

After the lottery has been run and the final housing list has been received from the Housing Office, residents who desire to may swap rooms. These swaps must be approved by everyone involved, including roommates, and must be published to official and given 24 hours to clear. Any swap involving a resident who cancels his housing before move-in will be nullified and all residents returned to their original rooms. Room swaps for the fall semester are not conducted over the summer vacation. Therefore, students have until the last day of class (Spring Semester) to request a swap. After this, students wishing to swap rooms for the fall must wait until after Registration Day, fall semester.

The RACs take no responsibility for maintaining or verifying bathroom gender preferences. If a resident has a strong aversion to sharing a bathroom with a given gender, it is that resident's responsibility to pick an appropriate room.

In special circumstances, it may become necessary for RACs to assign a student to a specific room. These requests must come from counseling or medical and have been approved by the Simmons House Team. These requests do not need to be made public and are not open for discussion. Resolution of specific situations is left to the RACs and house team.

Summer Rooming:

The Housing Office will provide a list of students assigned to Simmons for the summer. The RACs will assign each student to a room for the summer. Because of the temporary nature of these assignments as well as the time restriction between the list publication and end of spring term, no lottery will be held and specific assignments are left to the discretion of the RACs.

Seniors who are staying until graduation may stay in their rooms until they leave. Seniors who will be remaining for the rest of the summer may stay in their rooms as long as those rooms are available for the entire summer. For all other students, priority is given first to students who can move into their fall assignments. The next priority is for students remaining in their spring assignment. If neither of these conditions is met, preference is given to a nearby room of the appropriate size.

If a student's fall assignment is occupied by a senior who is leaving after graduation, that student should be given a temporary assignment in the closest proximity to their fall assignment as possible. Once the senior leaves, the student may move into their fall assignment. No other switches will be allowed after graduation.

Freshman Rooming:

Over the summer, the Housing Office will provide a list of freshman who have been assigned to Simmons. The RACs will be responsible for matching these freshman as well as writing a welcome letter. The freshmen should be paired such that all roommate requests are honored, and beyond that at the discretion of the RACs, with the understanding that these assignments are temporary and much of the necessary information is lacking.

Once the freshmen have received their permanent assignments to Simmons, the Freshmen Room Assignment process should take place. First priority goes to upperclassmen who selected doubles but not roommates in the House-Wide Spring Lottery. If these upperclassmen can find a mutually agreeable roommate among the freshmen, the freshman is removed from the remainder of the rooming process and assigned to the upperclassman's room. Failing this, preference sheets will be distributed to each freshman.

The priorities of the RACs will place the happiness of the freshmen collectively over the happiness of any individual freshman. Using their best judgment and the results of the preference sheets, the RACs will assign freshmen to the available doubles. Requests for a specific roommate will be honored. Squatting, however, is not allowed: no freshman will be given special consideration for their temporary assignment. Any requests based on special circumstances (medical, counseling, or otherwise) must come through the housing office and otherwise will not be considered at the time of the assignment process. The RACs will try to account for requests such as sharing bathrooms with a specific gender but cannot guarantee that such an assignment will be available.

Miscellaneous:

Any situation not covered by this policy should be resolved at the discretion of the RACs, who are encouraged to amend the policy if this should happen.

With the concurrence of the house team, any provision of this policy may be set aside under extraordinary circumstances. If possible, the RACs should identify and explain any such cases to the House Committee.

Claiming early sophomore standing will not change one's class year for the purposes of housing preferences.
